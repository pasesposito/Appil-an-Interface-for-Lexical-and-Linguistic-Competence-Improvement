Lesson 6: The Verb "To Do"

Welcome to Lesson 6! Today, we’ll focus on the verb "to do" in the present tense. This verb is crucial for forming questions, negative sentences, and emphasizing actions in English. It's one of the most commonly used verbs in everyday communication, making it essential for your language skills.

Understanding the Verb "To Do"

The verb "to do" serves two primary functions in English:

  • Main Verb: It can stand alone to indicate action.
  • Auxiliary Verb: It helps to form questions and negatives.

Here’s how it works with different subjects:

  • "Do": Used with the subjects I, you, we, they.
    • Example: "I do my homework every day." This sentence indicates a habitual action.
  • "Does": Used with the subjects he, she, it.
    • Example: "She does her chores every Saturday." This highlights a specific routine.

Let’s look at some examples in different sentence structures:

  • Affirmative Sentences: "I do my best." / "He does his work."
    • These sentences state a fact about someone's action.
  • Negative Sentences: "They don’t go to school today." / "She doesn’t play the piano."
    • Use “don’t” (do not) and “doesn’t” (does not) to indicate that an action is not happening.
  • Interrogative Sentences: "Do you like chocolate?" / "Does she live near here?"
    • These sentences are questions that seek information.
